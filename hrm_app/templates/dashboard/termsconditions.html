2. User Responsibilities

Employees must ensure that all information submitted through the HRM Portal is accurate and up to date.

  • Employees are responsible for verifying and maintaining accurate personal and contact information in the system.
  • Employees are required to mark clock-in and clock-out. Office working hours are from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  • The official working hours begin at 9:00 AM. Clock-ins after 9:10 AM will be marked as late. If an employee is late more than three times, it may be treated as a half-day
  • Employees may take leave on the day before or after a holiday. Such leave should be compensated; otherwise, it may result in a salary deduction.
  • Employees can check their total working hours after clock-out if the working hours are less than 8 hours due to late arrival or early departure especially on Saturdays, it will be marked as a half-day.
  • If an employee takes half-day leave but working hours less than 4 hours, the attendance will be marked as absent so every employee should responsible for maintaining their work schedule.
  • Hartal/Strike are not considered as a Holiday or Casual Leave, and employees should report for duty as usual or may work from home if required; the working day can also be compensated.

The company will not be responsible for issues caused by incorrect information submitted by employees.

3. Leave Management

The leave management system follows the company’s official Leave Policy. Employees submitting leave requests must follow the rules below.

  • Casual Leave (CL): Leave should be applied at least two days in advance. Employees must verify whether the leave request has been approved. A total of 12 casual leaves are available per year, and only 1 casual leave can be taken per month. If more than one casual leave is taken in a month, the additional leave will be considered unpaid.
  • Medical Leave: Medical leave may be applied after the leave has been taken and must be submitted within 30 days from the date of absence with valid medical documentation.
  • Emergency Leave (EL): Emergency leave may be applied within 30 days from the date of absence. Compensation is optional; if the leave is not compensated, salary may be deducted.
  • Submitting false or fabricated leave documents is considered a disciplinary offense.
